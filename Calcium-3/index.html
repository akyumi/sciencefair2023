    Brief Description

    Gecko-inspired adhesive mechanisms refer to the unique adhesive capabilities of geckos, which allow them to climb vertical surfaces and cling to ceilings using their specialized foot pads. These footpads contain millions of tiny hair-like structures called setae that utilize van der Waals forces to create adhesion, these adhesives are designed to have high adhesive strength, yet be easily detachable, which has potential applications in various fields, such as robotics, aerospace, and medical devices. (Jain et al., 2016)
