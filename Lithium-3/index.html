Introduction

Pollution is a growing problem within today’s world. Cars, factories, and other human activities release various pollutants into the atmosphere. One of these is carbon dioxide. While this gas is naturally produced by humans and animals, too much of it traps heat into the atmosphere, speeding up global warming and slowly destroying the environment.

The most common method of removing excess carbon dioxide from the atmosphere is through planting trees. This is because plants and trees can convert carbon dioxide into oxygen in a process called photosynthesis. Photosynthesis is very important in balancing an ecosystem, since plants can absorb the excess carbon dioxide produced by humans and animals and in turn produce oxygen for those humans and animals to breathe. For this reason, plants and trees are described as the "lungs of the earth."

However, plants aren't the only organisms that photosynthesize. Algae such as phytoplankton in the ocean can also photosynthesize and produce oxygen. In fact, more than half of the oxygen in the planet is produced by marine algae, while plants produce only about 20%, according to the U.S. National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA).

The Power of Algae

The main reason why algae produce so much oxygen is because of their high photosynthetic efficiency. Photosynthetic efficiency is basically how much light captures and successfully converts into useful products(Park, 2007), including oxygen. They are more efficient at photosynthesis because they have more pigments that capture solar energy and are better at concentrating carbon dioxide (Ma et al., 2023). The result of this is that algae have a photosynthetic efficiency of 10-20%, while plants have an efficiency of about 1-2%. That means algae can produce 10x more oxygen than plants do!

Algae also have several other benefits. They are more tolerant of carbon dioxide, meaning that they can be used in highly polluted areas. They have low light intensity requirements, they are not as constrained by land as plants are, and do not require herbicides or pesticides. Algae also grow at incredible rates—some species can double their growth in just 3.5 hours!

Algae In Action

A group of scientists from the University of Belgrade in Serbia have recently made an innovation that could prove the potential of algae in cleaning the atmosphere.

Dubbed by the Internet as "liquid trees", LIQUID3 is a "photo-bio reactor" that uses algae to clean the atmosphere, especially in highly polluted cities such as the Serbian capital of Belgrade. It is meant to be an innovative and practical way of reducing pollution, especially in cities where there isn't enough space to plant trees.

Conclusion

Algae holds a lot of potential in our fight against climate change and greenhouse gases. They produce 10 times more oxygen than plants, and thus capture 10 times more carbon dioxide from the atmosphere. They have several benefits that make them practical for use in highly-polluted urban areas where planting trees is not always feasible. They have already seen use all over the world, and they will be a key part of our fight against climate change in the near future.
